POL-40: Local Government Democracy and Rural Transformation in Nigeria (A Case Study of Jos South Local Government)

Findings of the Research Work

  1. Local Government democracy has influenced poverty reduction in rural areas.
  2. Public office holders at the local government are not often accountable to the people in local government areas.
  3. Local Government autonomy could be the solution for rural development and an effective local government system in Nigeria.
  4. The problems associated with Local Government democracy and rural transformations are the exclusion of rural populace in decision-making and leadership problems, respectively.
  5. Local Government administration in Nigeria can be an agent of rural transformation if effective democracy is practiced.

For a long period, rural populations, constituting about 75% of Nigeria's population, have endured conditions of poverty and deprivation. Efforts to improve the socio-economic life of these people have often failed. Challenges such as corruption, leadership problems, and the exclusion of rural communities in decision-making have contributed to this failure. The rural populace continues to hope for better changes.

To break this cycle of failure and effectively transform rural areas into catalysts for national development, there is an urgent need for continuous and sustained diversification of rural transformation strategies to enhance efficacy and effectiveness.
