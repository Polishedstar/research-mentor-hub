MGT-108: The Effect of Pricing Policy on Sales Turnover

Findings of the Research Work

Based on the data analyzed on Nasco, the following are the findings for this study on the effect of pricing policy on the sales turnover of Nasco foods:

  • Not all the staff of Nasco participate in its pricing decisions.
  • The factor that influences the price of Nasco products is the demand level of their products by customers.
  • The circumstances under which prices can be reviewed to achieve better sales turnover.
  • Nasco segments its markets based on behavioral aspects of consumers and geographical basis.
  • The prices of Nasco products are not the same in its different market segments.
  • The factors Nasco considers in setting its different prices for different market segments include peculiarity of the market, purchase occasion, and distribution distance.
  • A varied price for different market segments is the best for better sales turnover.
  • In determining the price of a product, the demand for such a product influences its price.
  • Nasco mostly uses market price approaches in pricing its products.
  • Nasco has never set a price just to recover its cost of production without considering buyers' reactions.
  • The circumstances in which Nasco should best review its product prices to achieve better sales are when there is an increase in the cost of production and poor demand levels.
  • The implications of poor pricing policy in Nasco's sales performance are a decrease in sales volume, low demand level, and a decline in profits.

Based on the collected data and the analysis of data collected, the following conclusions were made regarding findings that pricing is an important factor in management decision-making and good pricing policy determines the long-term success of an organization. Good pricing policy has a great impact on the sales turnover of Nasco products because it leads to high customer patronization and an increase in sales volume. If a company does not establish a good pricing policy, it becomes poor pricing policy, and its implications are drastic enough to the extent that it can lead to the closure of the company. The reasons include a decrease in sales volume, a decrease in product demand, and a decline in profits.
