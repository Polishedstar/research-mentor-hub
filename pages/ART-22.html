ART-22: Problems associated with Journalism Profession and Ethical Practices in the Nigerian Mass Media (A Case Study of PRTVC, Jos)

Findings of the Research Work

This study primarily focuses on "problems associated with journalism profession and ethical practices in the Nigerian mass media: A case study of PRTVC." The study aims to determine if journalists in PRTVC Jos have knowledge of the code of ethics of journalism practice. It also seeks to understand the extent to which the code of ethics is considered essential for professionalism in the practice of journalism in PRTVC Jos. The study investigates whether the knowledge of PRTVC journalists regarding the ethical code influences journalism practice. Furthermore, it examines whether journalists in PRTVC Jos adhere to the applicable code of ethics in journalism practice and identifies factors that influence media practitioners in PRTVC Jos to engage in unethical conduct.

The study includes a review of related literature, encompassing conceptual reviews where relevant concepts are explained and empirical reviews summarizing other researches in the field.

For the research design, the study adopts a survey method. Both primary and secondary data sources are used in the data collection process. The primary instrument for data collection is the questionnaire, and a simple random sampling technique is employed to distribute the questionnaires. Data collected from the field is analyzed using the Likert five-point scale method of data analysis.

The collected data is presented and analyzed, with responses categorized based on personal data such as sex, age, marital status, educational qualification, and occupation. These characteristics are presented in frequency distribution tables. The responses to relevant variables' questions are also analyzed and tabulated.
