POL-33: Conflict Management and Organizational Effectiveness (A Case Study of Jos South LGC)

Findings of the Research Work

Major findings of the study are summarized as follows:

  1. Firstly, it was discovered that workers' performance is the relationship between conflict management and organizational effectiveness.
  2. Secondly, the findings revealed that mismanagement and misappropriation are the most common and pervasive types of conflict, and the phenomenon has negatively affected the performance of most civil servants in Jos South local government area.
  3. Thirdly, it was discovered that the study provides an understanding of the pro-activeness and effectiveness of anti-graft agencies in tackling the critical problems of conflict in the public service, including the local government councils in Nigeria.
  4. Fourthly, the findings revealed that the menace of conflict in an organization has affected the performance of the public servants at the local government level, which culminated in low/poor productivity, reduction in government revenue generation, as well as primitive accumulation of wealth.
  5. Lastly, it was discovered that the problems of corruption and corrupt practices among personnel of local councils in Nigeria can be effectively tackled by stringent anti-corruption policies and laws, capital punishment sensitization campaigns, as well as strengthening anti-graft institutions. It was also revealed that proper and regular auditing, remuneration, rewards, as well as personnel supervision and discipline can tame the menace of corruption in the local government council and society at large.
