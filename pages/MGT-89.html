MGT-89: The Contribution of Small Scale Businesses on Poverty Alleviation

Findings of the Research Work

  • Financial problems have been the major and most serious impediment to small-scale enterprises towards buttressing the growth and development of industries in Plateau State, particularly in Jos North.
  • Nigeria's policies and programs towards small-scale firms have always been efficient but not effective.
  • It is now a known fact that small-scale business is the bedrock of any meaningful economic growth and development, and human beings play that important role.
  • High levels of illiteracy by the existing entrepreneurs who are, in one way or another, not mentally balanced to carry out some business programs caused by educational impediment.
  • This report also found out that the inability to source for loans from banks which require collateral, poverty, and corruption hinders the growth and development of the economy.
  • This research also found out that small-scale businesses serve as a means for entrepreneurs and employees to be self-reliant. Therefore, every economy depends largely on the contribution of small-scale business.

This research has provided procedures necessary to start up a small-scale business. Also, the problems faced by SMEs are examined and highlighted, as a result of which solutions have been provided. These solutions are geared toward addressing the problems hindering the progress of small-scale business.

Because of the major roles that small businesses have played in the development of the economy and also in alleviating poverty, the researcher recommends that there should be a provision of capitals through loans. It was also observed that NGOs and individuals need to encourage.

The researcher, therefore, concludes by saying that small-scale business has contributed to the growth and development of the economy and has also helped in poverty alleviation.
