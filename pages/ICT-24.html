ICT-24: Assessment of the Contribution of Office Technology and Management Graduates to the Development of Polytechnic Education

Findings of the Research Work

The study was undertaken to identify the contributions of office technology and management graduates to the development of Polytechnic education. To facilitate the conduct of the study, three purposes of the study were postulated from which three research questions were raised. The study adopted the descriptive survey research design, the population for the study consisted of 120 students of office technology and management, Plateau State Polytechnic, Barkin Ladi. The entire study population was adopted for the study, since the population was not too large. The questionnaire was the instrument used for data collection, the instrument was subjected to face and content validation by the researcher's supervisor. Data collected for the study were analyzed using the mean rating on a four-point Likert scale.

The study revealed the following findings among others:

Contributions of Secretaries in Record Management:

  • Record management is responsible for the efficient and systematic control of records.
  • Record management serves as the evidence of an organization's activities.
  • Record management is essential to the survival of an organization.
  • Record management contributes to the development of polytechnic education.
  • Record management helps in the reduction or mitigation of risk.

Contributions of Secretaries in Typing Documents:

  • Typing is a process of writing or inputting text by pressing keys.
  • Typing can be done on a typewriter, computer keyboard, cell phone, or calculator.
  • Typing of documents is essential to every organization.
  • Typing of documents contributes to the development of polytechnic education.
  • Typing of documents is done so that documents can be arranged in an orderly and organized manner.

Challenges:

  • Secretaries being blamed for everything that goes wrong.
  • Time management.
  • Understanding the business in Plateau State Polytechnic.
  • Dealing with difficult personalities.
  • Adaptability.
