ENV-14: Building Collapse in Nigeria: Design or Construction Problem (A Case Study of Lagos-Island, Lagos Stata)

Findings of the Research Work

After a detailed analysis of data gathered from the three categories of respondents, including the Lagos State Ministry of Housing, Real Estate Developers, and Professional teams in the building industry, the following are the summary of findings:

  1. The analysis revealed that the majority of the respondents are male and have witnessed and are aware of incidents of building collapse in Lagos.

  2. The respondents also agreed to the fact that the degree of the effect of building collapse is Very High and, as such, has caused a lot of problems for the government as well as the professionals concerned. It was also agreed that the prevalence of building collapse in Lagos is high in nature. The government and professionals are making efforts to eradicate it through public enlightenment and proper site supervision.

The three categories of respondents, i.e., the government Ministry of Housing, real estate developers, and professional teams in the built environment, identified the following as causes of building collapse:

  • Structural defect
  • Faulty foundation
  • Lack of maintenance
  • Poor workmanship
  • Sub-standard materials
  • Design error
  • Natural hazards
  • Inexperienced professionals
  • Hasty construction
  • Greed
  • Non-adherence to building codes
  • Overloading
  • Tendency to cheat
  • Corruption
  • Inadequate steel reinforcement
  • No soil test, etc.
