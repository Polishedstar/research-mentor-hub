MGT-69: The Impact of Industrial Relationships on Organizational Productivity

Findings of the Research Work

Employees are held to occupy a strategic role in corporate success. It was found that there was a relationship between employees and the performance of the organization. All employees made contributions to the organization according to the duties assigned to them. The company seeks to have well-motivated, trained, and professional employees as it was found that the company employed more professional employees than unprofessional employees. There is a high level of employee turnover because of a lack of motivation, inefficient communication, poor working conditions, and a lack of employee participation.

Findings on the Level of Organizational Performance for the Last Five Years

The organizational performance has increased for the last five years. According to respondents, the performance is a result of a good reputation, increased market share, and a high annual turnover, indicating that performance has improved. Despite the high level of performance, the organization still realizes poor performance due to poor relationships between employees and management.

Findings on the Relationship Between Employees and Organizational Performance

The study findings show that a strong positive correlation exists between employees and organizational performance since r=0.8, indicating that employees have a significant influence on the performance of the organization.

The result from Pearson correlation shows that there is a strong positive relationship between employees and organizational performance. The study findings revealed that poor performance is a result of poor working conditions, a lack of motivation, ineffective communication, and a lack of employee participation. Employee relations have a significant impact on organizational performance; if the relationship is poor, organizational performance will be negatively affected.
