POL-25: Role of Traditional Rulers in Conflict Prevention and Mediation in Kaduna North Local Government

Findings of the Research Work

The essence of this research work is to assess the role and prospect of traditional rulers in crisis management. The chapter one of the work gave a brief introduction of traditional institutional institution, problems were stated, research questions were asked, aims and objectives were presented, and the scope of the study was also presented. The chapter two of this work presented the review of traditional institutions in historical perspective with structural functionalism adapted for the research work. While the chapter three and four for revealed the techniques adopted at arriving at the findings and the presentation and analysis of data using tabular form.

From the study, the researcher was able to know that traditional rulers affect local government administration immensely. It was discovered that traditional rulers have a positive impact in promoting governance and crisis management in Ryom local government area.

This study is aimed at finding out the role and prospects of traditional rulers in crisis management with specific emphasis on Ryom local government area of Plateau state. This study revealed precisely that traditional rulers are the major agents in transforming the attitude of rural people in the area of crisis management and conflict resolution.

Based on the findings, the researcher has come to the conclusion that traditional rulers have made a positive impact in crisis management to a degree, but there is room for improvement as there is still dissatisfaction from some segments of the population.
