ACC-18: The Effect of Accounting Standards on the Quality of Financial Reporting in Nigeria (A Case Study of First Bank Nigeria Plc, Jos Branch)

Findings of the Research Work

The research work aimed to uncover the benefits that Nigerian companies can attain by adopting and implementing the International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) for the preparation and reporting of their financial statements. Two hypotheses were assumed and tested using questionnaires to gather data from chartered accountants, auditors, managers, and investors in Jos North Local Government of Plateau State. The study's outcomes revealed the following:

  1. The adoption of IFRS will increase the level of confidence of global investors and investment analysts in the financial statements of companies in Nigeria.
  2. The adoption of IFRS is an effective tool for enhancing the uniformity and comparability of financial statements of companies in Nigeria.
  3. Companies that have adopted IFRS will be able to generate more funds from foreign sources.
  4. While there are challenges hindering the successful adoption and implementation of IFRS, the government has implemented adequate measures to address these issues.

Ultimately, the adoption and implementation of IFRS is expected to increase the Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) inflow in Nigeria.
