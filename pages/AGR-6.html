AGR-6: Assessment of factors affecting Agricultural Production in Bassa Local Government Area

Findings of the Research Work

This section summarizes the study, which results on the impact of UA on households’ income, access to foodstuffs in Bassa LGA, and the extent to which it is acting as a livelihood strategy to households that are prone to food insecurity.

Urban agriculture participants in Bassa LGA had the largest proportions of the old-aged, female-headed, singles or divorced, widowed, lowly educated, and informally employed households with higher dependency ratios and household sizes compared to non-participants.

The study revealed that UA contributes up to 74 percent of households’ income in Bassa. This amounts to US$820.35 annually, which is statistically greater than 50%. Income saved by the participants, on average from weekly consumption from subsistence in-season, was amounting to US$12.70.

Bassa LGA residents’ maize harvest lasted 4.14 months on average. Consumption of sweet potatoes, peas, pumpkin fruits, pumpkin leaves, and beans was statistically higher than that of the non-participants in-season. In the off-season, only consumption of vegetables was significantly higher than that of non-participants.
