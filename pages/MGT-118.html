MGT-118: Quality Service and Customers Satisfaction

Findings of the Research Work

Training is a process through which staff of an organization acquire additional skills, abilities, attitudes, and knowledge in their discipline or profession to enhance the services provided to various customers.

There are diverse ways of acquiring services in an organization. Some organizations prefer the most popularly used system of providing direct services, while others opt for indirect services due to time and space constraints.

The importance attached to quality service and customer satisfaction is immense, and organizations, whether public or private, have embraced it to help them achieve their set goals.

This report also found that there are major factors affecting quality services and customer satisfaction. These factors include the manner of approach, attitudes of customers, failure to recognize customers' needs, nagging customers, and other undesirable behaviors.

Throughout this report, the central topic of discussion has been quality services and customer satisfaction. The following conclusions can be drawn from the research findings:

  • There are several staff members who have served for long periods without promotions.
  • Lack of incentives and working relationships, as well as inadequate funding and low levels of motivation in the organization, have been identified as issues.
  • Lack of proper training seminars has given the management an unfavorable image.
  • Uncoordinated banking ethics have also been observed.

Ultimately, the success of management in an organization depends on the staff and employees. A vital concluding remark to note is that this research project has taken an empirical approach to the study of quality services. It addresses common problems associated with theoretical considerations and empirical analysis, providing an effective management tool or model for organizations.
