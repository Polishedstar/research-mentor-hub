ACC-19: An Evaluation of the Management of Bad and Doubtful Debts as it affects Profitability in Commercial Banks (A Case Study of Eco-Bank)

Findings of the Research Work

  1. Eco-bank grants credit facilities to its customers to help them overcome financial insufficiency.
  2. There are specific customers or individuals who default in the repayment of loans or advances.
  3. Collateral security is considered necessary when granting loans to customers to mitigate defaults.
  4. Eco-bank commonly accepts landed property as collateral security to safeguard the loans given to customers.
  5. Employees below the rank of a supervisor are not authorized to grant loans due to the complexity and guidelines involved.
  6. Banks must comply with C.B.N. guidelines, and Eco-bank strictly adheres to them.
  7. Default in loan repayment is a significant issue in the banking sector, and measures need to be taken to address this problem.
  8. Eco-bank has established mechanisms for the recovery of loans, although efforts are ongoing to improve the recovery process.
