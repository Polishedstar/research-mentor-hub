MGT-91: Effect of After-Sale on the Purchase of Industrial Goods with Particular Reference to Nasco

Findings of the Research Work

Based on the data analyzed in previous chapters, the researcher discovered the following:

  • It was revealed that after-sale service is the marketing activity performed after a sale has been made, a means of deep insight into customers, purchased goods, and bedrock of customers’ relationship with the organization.
  • It was discovered that after-sale service influences the purchase of industrial goods because it leads to effective utilization of purchased goods. After-sale service has provided customer satisfaction, customers' relaxation, and continuous patronage, thereby encouraging customers’ loyalty.
  • It also revealed that after-sale service reduces customers' cost of maintenance on purchased industrial goods through preventive maintenance.
  • After-sale service increases the profitability and market share of any competitive market, as increased patronage of industrial goods leads to higher profitability and market share.

From the above findings, the impact of after-sale service cannot be over-emphasized. Companies need after-sale service to stay ahead of their competition. A well-executed after-sale service should increase the relationship between the company and its customers, aligning with the company’s objectives and goals. A deep insight into customers' purchased goods and the bedrock of customers’ relationship with the organization was one among many findings.

In conclusion, despite the challenges faced by after-sale service in the purchase of industrial goods, the service has a significant impact on customers and the organization. This impact includes customer satisfaction, cost reduction, and acting as feedback for an organization's products and services.
