ACC-45: Effect of Tax Avoidance and Evasion on Revenue Generation in Plateau State

Findings of the Research Work

Based on the analysis of this study, summaries of discoveries drawn are presented here below:

  1. Tax avoidance and evasion have effects on income generation in Nigeria.
  2. The effects of tax avoidance and evasion on income generation of Nigeria are that tax avoidance and evasion lower government revenue generation; tax avoidance and evasion lower gross domestic products of Nigeria; tax avoidance and evasion lead to low employment rates in Nigeria, and tax avoidance and evasion generate investment distortion in the form of the purchase of assets exempted from tax or undervalued for tax purposes.
  3. People evade and avoid taxes due to high tax rates or tax burden, because they are not willing to pay tax, because of their low income, and because of using legal tax.
  4. The effects of tax avoidance and evasion on economic development of Nigeria are that tax avoidance and evasion have a negative and significant impact on the growth of the Nigeria Economy; it lowers government revenue and leads to low employment rates in Nigeria; it has a negative effect on the Gross Domestic Products of Nigeria, and tax avoidance and evasion generate investment distortion in the form of the purchase of assets exempted from tax or undervalued for tax purposes.
  5. The problems of tax avoidance and evasion in Plateau as well as the country at large are the tax revenue loss which may cause serious damage to the proper performance of the public sector, threatening its capability to finance public expenditure, unemployment/low gross domestic products, and low economic growth and development.
  6. There are solutions to tax avoidance and evasion in Nigeria.
