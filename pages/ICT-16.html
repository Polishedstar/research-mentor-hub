ICT-16: Identification of Facebook Skills needed by Students of Office Technology and Management

Findings of the Research Work

The study aimed to identify the Facebook skills needed by students of Office Technology and Management. Three specific purposes and research questions were formulated to guide the study. The research was conducted at Plateau State Polytechnic, Barkin Ladi, and data were collected from ND II students of the Office Technology and Management department using questionnaires. Mean ratings on a five-point Likert scale were used to analyze the data collected from the respondents.

Research Question 1: The study revealed that the Facebook skills needed by OTM students include:

  • Creating a Facebook account
  • Ability to sign in and sign out of the Facebook account
  • Typing skills
  • Editing and sending messages using Facebook
  • Buying and selling things through the Facebook platform
  • Ability to download videos, audios, images, etc., on Facebook
  • Ability to search for people, places, assignments, etc.
  • Ability to create mini-chat groups successfully

Research Question 2: The study found that Facebook serves as a tool for:

  • Distraction from academic excellence
  • Promotion of fake news
  • Social vices
  • Hate speech among students
  • Destructive impact on Nigerian culture
  • Impact on the English language
  • Promoting examination malpractice

Research Question 3: The study suggests that students should:

  • Be disciplined to allocate time for leisure and study
  • Verify information seen on Facebook before using it
  • Avoid visiting Facebook pages and audio-visual contents promoting social vices
  • Avoid making hate speeches on Facebook
  • Avoid replacing Nigerian culture with Western culture seen on Facebook
  • Always type and spell words in full
  • Avoid using shortforms
  • Avoid using Facebook as a tool for examination malpractice
