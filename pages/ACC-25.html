ACC-25: Effect of Value Added Tax on Consumption (A Case Study of Grand Cereals and Oil Mills, Jos)

Findings of the Research Work

This research work was set out with the main aim of ascertaining the effect of Value Added Tax on Consumption in Grand Cereals and Oil Mills, Jos.

The tool of data collection employed was the questionnaire, and the results are as follows:

  1. Value added tax has an impact on consumption.
  2. The collection of value added tax is effective.
  3. Value added tax generates more income for the government than sales tax.
  4. The amount realized from value added tax is not properly accounted for.
  5. There are measures put in place by the government for effective VAT collection.
  6. VAT application is effectively implemented.
  7. Fluctuations in VAT income affect consumption.
  8. Training and retraining of FIRS staff will improve VAT collection.
  9. Direct payment of VAT proceeds will go a long way in effective collection.
