ENV-24: Evaluation of Rural Development Strategies in Nigeria: A Case Study of Idheze Community in Isoko-South Local Government Area, Delta State

Findings of the Research Work

From the research conducted in the study area, the following summary has been reached:

  • The existing Rural Development strategies are not functional.
  • The strategies in use in the study area are not sustainable.
  • Development does not meet the desired needs of the people.
  • The strategy of rural development is not community-driven, neither is it self-sustaining.
  • The government and oil companies are major contributors to Rural Development.
  • Provided development in the form of rural development lacks the participation of the inhabitants of the study area (public participation).
  • Provided facilities are poorly maintained.
  • The inhabitants are not happy about the rate of development.
  • Problems militating against Rural development range from political elite, corruption, nonchalant attitude of leaders, to community youths' agitation.
