HLT-13: Role of Welfare Department in Child Adoption in Jos North LGA

Findings of the Research Work

The summary of this research case study shows that:

  1. There is a disagreement about whether there is a significant improvement in the policy of child adoption by the social welfare department of Jos North Local Government Area.
  2. There was a majority agreement that there is no significant improvement in the policy of child adoption.
  3. Ignorance is not always a contributing factor that makes people in the area not report cases that are likely to be taken up by the social welfare department in terms of child adoption.
  4. Cultural practices and beliefs deter some people from reporting cases that need adoption.
  5. The social welfare department has not done much to enlighten people on its role in child adoption.
  6. The policy implementers have not carried out their responsibilities effectively to attract the attention of the inhabitants to encourage them to report cases related to adoption.
  7. The Local Government's child adoption policy has not significantly affected the rural dwellers.
  8. Social service workers have not been doing well enough to arouse the attention of local communities in child adoption practice.
  9. The Local Government authorities have not invested significant human and material resources in social welfare services.
  10. The majority of the inhabitants confirmed that their communities look at adoption from a positive perspective.
