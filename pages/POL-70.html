POL-70: Good Governance and Socio-Economic Transformation in Plateau State (A Case Study of Mangu LGA)

Findings of the Research Work

The study explored the implications of good governance on the socio-economic transformation of the grassroots. The roles of governance in the local government include transparency, accountability, continuity, and efficiency, respectively.

Moreover, the study indicates that good governance enhances the provision of social amenities such as roads, hospitals, schools, markets, electricity, water, agriculture, among others. But to the people of the local government, the provision has been below expectation as a result of corruption, wastage of resources, poor planning, and political interference.

Other challenges include leaders using their positions as an opportunity to enrich themselves instead of serving the people. Furthermore, the security of life and properties of citizens is not well protected, whereas equality and justice are not taken into consideration as a result of poor governance.

Good governance is no doubt necessary for the achievement of social, political, and economic progress in any society. It is the means through which the state can effectively address the allocation and management of resources to enable it to respond to the collective good of society without discrimination. Failure to enhance good governance can result in widespread corruption and abuse of due process. The results are a lack of accountability and widespread poverty for the vast majority of the people, making them vulnerable to easy manipulation and exploitation by the ruling class.

Consequently, to promote good governance and accountability in Nigeria, there is a need to ensure that the basic structures of government, such as the executive, the legislature, and the judiciary, live up to their responsibilities. In addition, the leadership must demonstrate the capacity, courage, will, and sincerity of purpose in tackling the identified challenges. Finally, and fundamentally, there is a need for radical structural changes in the operative mode of production in Nigeria to ensure inclusive, rather than exclusive governance, justice, and the rule of law for all.
