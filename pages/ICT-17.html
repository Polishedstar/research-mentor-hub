ICT-17: Computer Application Skills Required by Professional Secretaries for Job Success in Government Organizations (A Case Study of Industrial Training Fund Headquaters, Jos)

Findings of the Research Work

The study aimed to assess the computer application skills needed by secretaries in the modern workplace at Industrial Training Fund (ITF) Headquarters. Four research purposes and four research questions were formulated to guide the study. The research design adopted for the study was descriptive survey. The population consisted of sixty (60) staff at the Industrial Training Fund, Jos, with a sample size of forty (40) respondents. Data collection was done using questionnaires, and the instrument was validated for face and content by the researcher's supervisor. Data collected were analyzed using the mean rating on a four-point Likert scale.

The study revealed several findings, including:

  • Word processing, desktop publishing, internet, communication, and webspace skills are among the various skills needed by professional secretaries.
  • Computer application skills have significant impacts on professional secretaries, including enhancing organizational abilities, professional communication skills, and telephone call handling.
  • Importance of computer application skills on professional secretaries' job success in government organizations, including staying updated with current trends, preparation for advancing technology, improved communication, enhanced professionalism, connectivity, and business correspondence.
  • Challenges faced by professional secretaries, such as the risk of the extinction of secretarial work due to technological advancements, blame for organizational issues, dealing with difficult personalities, coping with changes in the organization, difficulties in using office equipment, and low motivation due to low salaries and remunerations.
