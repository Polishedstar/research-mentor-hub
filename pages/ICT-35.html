ICT-35: A Survey of the problems associated with Teaching and Learning of Shorthand

Findings of the Research Work

The study shows that the following factors contribute to the challenges in teaching and learning shorthand:

  • Lack of interest in shorthand
  • Perception of shorthand being difficult
  • Computer-based subjects taking precedence over shorthand
  • Suggestions to remove shorthand from the syllabus
  • Nonchalant attitude of students
  • Large population of students in class
  • Lack of adequate instructional equipment

Lack of interest in shorthand is a problem in the teaching and learning of shorthand, as observed through questionnaire responses. Respondents expressed a preference for improving their knowledge of shorthand.

Additionally, the lack of adequate teaching equipment is a challenge. When a teacher cannot clearly and confidently illustrate to students during teaching, it becomes difficult to impart knowledge optimally. When learners do not show a favorable attitude towards learning the course, it remains problematic.
