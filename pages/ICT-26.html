ICT-26: Office Work Skills Needed by Secretaries in Modern Workplace

Findings of the Research Work

The study aimed to assess the office work skills needed by secretaries in the modern workplace at the Industrial Training Fund (ITF) Headquarters. The findings are based on the results of the data analysis.

It was discovered that word processing skills needed by secretaries in a modern workplace include:

  • Typing using appropriate finger techniques
  • Formatting skills
  • Use of computers
  • Organization and record-keeping in the workplace

The study further revealed that communication office work skills needed by secretaries in a modern workplace include:

  • Composing routine correspondence
  • Communicating on behalf of their managers
  • Handling telephone calls efficiently and competently
  • Writing business letters, memos, and reports
  • Ability to hear, speak, and write effectively

It was also discovered that management and supervisory office work skills needed by secretaries in a modern workplace include:

  • Supervising jobs and duties assigned to various office workers
  • Controlling the use of office stationery
  • Attending and taking minutes of meetings
  • Contributing to improved productivity, growth, and development
  • Exhibiting qualities of a role model
  • Attention to details
  • Being initiative and driven

The study further revealed that human and interpersonal skills needed by secretaries in a modern workplace include:

  • Accepting responsibility for mistakes
  • Exhibiting moral integrity
  • Obeying authorities
  • Exhibiting good public relations in the workplace
  • Being clear, friendly, and accommodating
