AGR-5: Factors Influencing Declining Agricultural Production among Small Scale Farmers in Bassa Local Government Area

Findings of the Research Work

The first objective of this study was to determine the influence of socio-cultural factors on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA. Socio-cultural factors were operationalized as values, tradition, and social groups. Majority (88.80%) of small-scale farmers surveyed had poor socio-cultural factors with an average agricultural production of 44.02%, (S = 7.34). Socio-cultural factors were found to have a significant influence on the agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA; (F [1, 78] = 5.11, p = .027), and to account for 4.9% of the variance in the agricultural production of small-scale farmers (R = .248, R2 adj = .049, p = .027). Hence values, tradition, and social groups were determined to have a significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA.

Secondly, this study determined the influence of socio-economic factors on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA. Socio-economic factors were operationalized as income, education, occupation, and wealth. Majority (76.25%) of small-scale farmers surveyed were found to have poor socio-economic factors, with an average agricultural production of 44.08% (S = 7.38). The socio-economic factors of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA were adjudged to be poor. The study found that socio-economic factors had no significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA; (F [1, 78] = 1.85, p = .177). Hence income, education, occupation, and wealth have no significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA.

Lastly, this study assessed the influence of agricultural technology adoption on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA. Agricultural technology adoption was characterized as irrigation system, plows, and knowledge transfer. Majority (63.80%) of small-scale farmers surveyed were found to have poor agricultural technology adoption with an average agricultural production of 42.73% (S = 7.13). Agricultural technology adoption of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA was found to be generally poor. The study established that agricultural technology adoption had a significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA; (F [1, 78] = 11.06, p = .001), and that it accounted for 11.30% of the agricultural production of small-scale farmers (R = .352, R2 adj = .113, p = .001). Hence irrigation system, plows, and knowledge transfer have a significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers in Bassa LGA.

Therefore, the study established that;

  1. Socio-cultural factors have a significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers of Bassa LGA; F (1, 78) = 5.11, p = .027.
  2. Socio-economic status has no significant influences on agricultural production of small-scale farmers of Bassa LGA; F (1, 78) = 1.85, p = .177.
  3. Agricultural technology adoption has a significant influence on agricultural production of small-scale farmers of Bassa LGA; F (1, 78) = 11.06, p = .001.
