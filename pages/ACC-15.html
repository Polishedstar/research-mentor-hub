ACC-15: Bank Failure and its Effect on the Development of Nigerian Economy

Findings of the Research Work

As a result of the research conducted, the following complementary measures of bank failure in Nigeria and complementary solutions to bank failure have been discovered:

  1. Economic Impact: Bank failure has had a negative impact on the development of the Nigerian economy.
  2. Assets and Liability Management: Poor assets and liability management by banks have negatively affected the development of the Nigerian economy.
  3. Banking Reforms: The recent banking reforms in Nigeria have brought about positive changes in the economy.
  4. Recapitalization: Recapitalization of the banking sector is expected to address the growing problem of bank failures in the country.
  5. Role of Banks: Banks hold a unique position for effective growth and development in Nigeria.
  6. Loss of Confidence: Poor banking systems lead to a loss of confidence among both the public and private investors.

In general, bank failure in Nigeria is attributed to corruption, poor banking management, inadequate asset management, weak capital bases of banks, excessive government interference, unqualified and untrained manpower, non-repayment of loans by customers, and ineffective marketing strategies.
