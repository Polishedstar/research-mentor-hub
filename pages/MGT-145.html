MGT-145: Evaluation Performance as a Tool for Improving Productivity in the Private Sector

Findings of the Research Work

So far, this study has been able to look into performance evaluation as a tool for improving productivity in the private sector. With the responses obtained from the questionnaires, it is evident that performance evaluation plays a significant role in the private sector, especially in improving productivity, the quality of output, and worker motivation. It was discovered that performance evaluation can serve as a means of determining how fit a worker is to carry out an assignment task and which task can be assigned to them.

The study was able to establish a relationship between productivity and performance evaluation, finding that productivity is directly proportional to performance. Performance evaluation, as carried out in the private sector, may sometimes prove ineffective. However, during the course of the study, it was discovered that such ineffectiveness arises due to poor implementation of performance evaluation tools by managers or personnel assigned to such tasks. If carried out effectively, properly, and accurately, the result is high productivity in the private sector.

In conclusion, performance evaluation is a highly effective tool for improving the productivity of an organization. It reveals past performance against set targets as a means of improving future job performance and supports the determination of salary actions. Performance evaluation serves as a yardstick for determining the staff members who need training to enhance their productivity in achieving the organizational goals.
