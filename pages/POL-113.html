POL-113: Migration and Conflict Escalation in Plateau State (A Case Study of Jos North LGA)

Findings of the Research Work

This study has examined the causes and effects of ethnic-religious conflict escalation in Jos North Local Government Area and its implications for national integration. It is pertinent to provide a summary of the major findings:

  1. There is a significant correlation between ethnic-religious conflict management and national integration. This is evident in areas such as peace, corporate existence, socio-economic development, and effective policy implementation throughout the state.
  2. Government and security agents have performed poorly in addressing critical problems associated with ethnic-religious conflicts in Plateau State. Approximately 72.8% of the findings indicate ongoing attacks in many communities, despite government and security agents' efforts.
  3. Ethnic-religious conflicts have threatened peaceful coexistence and sustainable development in Jos North Local Government Area. About 76% of the findings reveal that various crises hinder harmonious relations between Christians and Muslims in Plateau State, particularly in Jos North Local Government Area.
  4. Multiple factors are responsible for the frequent ethnic-religious conflicts in Jos North Local Government Area, including indigene-settler issues, political participation/representation, Islamization agendas, economic control by the Hausa-Fulani, ownership, indigenship, and migration, among others.
  5. Government, community leaders, and NGOs have adopted various strategies to manage ethnic-religious conflicts effectively in Jos North. These strategies include security forces deployment, peace initiatives like summits and conferences, youth sensitization, mediation, conciliation, negotiation, and bargaining.

However, the findings also indicate that about 53% of these measures have not been effective, as evidenced by the frequent and continuing crises in Jos North and Plateau State as a whole.
