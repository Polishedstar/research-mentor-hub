ACC-22: The Impact of Internal Control System on Revenue Generation (A Case Study of Power Holding Company of Nigeria)

Findings of the Research Work

Based on the analysis of data collected and interpreted, the research summarizes the findings as follows:

  1. The internal control system in operation has some impact on service delivery and revenue generation. The company adopts various types and techniques of internal control to meet customer electricity demand, provide credible bills based on proper tariff, and generate revenue.
  2. The company ensures that no one individual can control both the recording function and transaction processing procedures, thus segregating their duties.
  3. The internal audit system at the company promotes compliance with set policies and enhances the accuracy and reliability of recorded transactions.
  4. Weaknesses in the internal control system can create opportunities for collusion, fraud, revenue loss, embezzlement, assets conversion, and corruption.
  5. Periodic evaluation of the internal control system by an external auditor is used to effectively assess control activities and detect fraudulent practices.
