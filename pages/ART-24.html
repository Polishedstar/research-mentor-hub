ART-24: Challenges of Campus Radio Broadcast Station in Nigeria (A Case Study of Foundation Rock 98.9Fm Jos)

Findings of the Research Work

This study primarily focuses on "challenges of campus radio broadcast station in Nigeria: A case study of Foundation Rock 98.9 FM Jos." The study aims to determine whether Foundation Rock 98.9 FM Jos programs are effective and beneficial to the people of Jos in their quest for information. It also seeks to understand if Foundation Rock 98.9 FM Jos is contributing to the community through the content of their programs. The study also investigates the challenges associated with Foundation Rock 98.9 FM Jos campus radio station. Furthermore, it examines the factors responsible for the challenges facing Foundation Rock 98.9 FM Jos and the extent to which these challenges are hindering the effective operation of Rock 98.9 FM Jos campus radio station.

A

The study includes a review of related literature, encompassing conceptual reviews where relevant concepts are explained and empirical reviews summarizing other researches in the field.

For the research design, the study adopts a survey method. Both primary and secondary data sources are used in the data collection process. The primary instrument for data collection is the questionnaire, and a simple random sampling technique is employed to distribute the questionnaires. Data collected from the field is analyzed using the Likert five-point scale method of data analysis.

The collected data is presented and analyzed, with responses categorized based on personal data, including sex, age, marital status, educational qualification, and occupation. These characteristics are presented in frequency distribution tables. The responses to the question or relevant variables questions are also analyzed and tabulated.

The results revealed responses related to the challenges of a campus radio broadcast station in Nigeria. It particularly discovered certain challenges of Foundation Rock 98.9 FM Jos, such as Power, Funding, poor management structure, and inadequate staff/personnel.
