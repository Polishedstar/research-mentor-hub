POL-91: Impact of Bureaucracu on Civil Service Performancer (A Case Study of Ministry of Finance, Jos)

Findings of the Research Work

Based on the data analyzed in chapter four, the following findings were arrived at:

  1. Bureaucracy encourages high productivity in the civil service.
  2. The Ministry of Finance in Jos provides good incentives for both superiors and subordinates.
  3. Proper communication enhances high productivity in the Ministry of Finance.
  4. Bureaucracy leads to efficient service delivery in the Ministry.
  5. Delegation of responsibilities enhances employee performance in the Ministry of Works, Housing, and Transport in Jos, Plateau State.
  6. Wage increase is the best way of providing incentives to civil servants.
  7. Adherence to rules and regulations improves performance in an organization.
  8. Chain of command leads to greater efficiency in the civil service.
  9. The performance of staff of the Plateau State Ministry of Finance before the implementation of the new minimum wage was good.
  10. Bureaucracy has improved the relationship between staff of the Plateau State Ministry of Finance greatly.

It is obvious that the minimum wage increase of the Nigerian civil servant is to enable them to perform effectively and efficiently. The policies regulating wage increases should create a conducive environment for the civil servant to operate.

For any employer to enjoy maximum output from their employees, they should be able to pay a good wage or abide by the statutory provisions of the minimum wage for Nigerian civil servants and the organized private sector.

The research findings have been able to establish a relationship between the new minimum wage and the Nigerian economy, as shown by the results of this research.
