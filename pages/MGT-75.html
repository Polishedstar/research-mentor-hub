MGT-75: Impact of Quality Control on Maintenance of Product Standard

Findings of the Research Work

In the course of the study, some facts were established based on findings and the analysis of such findings. From the data presented in table (1), drawn from responses to question (1), 76% of the staff strongly agreed that quality control has an impact on maintaining product standards in the organization. Likewise, in table (2), it was determined that effective quality control satisfies customers' needs.

Furthermore, it was ascertained from table (3) that 64% of the respondents believed that the turnover rate of an organization is determined by the significance of quality control. Similarly, in table (4), the respondents confirmed that staff training in the organization has a very positive effect on the product. Quality control personnel training is predominantly carried out through induction courses to acquaint them with established standards. In table (5), 98% agreed that it reduces the level of scrap value and minimizes the rate of wastage of materials used in producing a product.

From the analysis, the two alternative hypotheses (H1) were accepted while the null hypotheses were rejected. By accepting the alternative hypotheses, it is evident that the success of any manufacturing firm depends on the quality control department, which requires a sound scientific knowledge of principles and principles of processing, manufacturing, storage, and distribution.

Achieving high quality does not happen by accident. The production process must be properly managed to achieve quality standards. To establish and run an effective quality control unit, the quality control manager requires managerial skills and a sound knowledge of science and technology.

Quality has to be something that is considered from the very beginning and built into everything that a business or organization does. Planning carefully, monitoring your work, and constant re-evaluation and adjustment are all extremely important. You don't ensure quality by catching mistakes before they reach the customer. You ensure it by setting up a system in which you don't make the mistakes to begin with.
